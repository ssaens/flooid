Title, Summary and Team Members

Fluid Simulation


Summary

We will be building a 3D particle based fluid simulator that can render realistic demo scenes of fluid interaction and movement. Ideally, the fluid will also interact with physical objects with different bouyancies.

Team Members

Dillon Yao

Tony Situ

Problem Description

3D particle based fluid simulation is a widely explored field in computer graphics with a variety of techinical challenges and potential optimizations. Simulating realistic fluid motion has to take into account an array of issues in terms of computational complexity and physical constraints such as enforcing fluid properties e.g. incompressibility, surface tension, and vorticity. Developing and improving fluid simulation methods would have a huge impact in a variety of outside of immediately obvious applications in computer graphics. Simulating fluids is important to industries like animation, civil engineering, chemical engineering, materials engineering, etc. Our approach to tackling the challenge of fluid simulation will follow research papers published on the subject that identify various mathematical methods that best emulate the properties of fluids and to deal with computation complexity we will be leveraging the parallelizability of particle computation in fluids.

Goals and Deliverables

Planned Deliverables

At the end of this project, we expect to have a 3D position based particle fluid simulator. We will create a demo scene showing a classic fluid tank with static objects (unaffected by physics) and show how the fluid interacts with these objects

The quality of the simulation can be measured by mirroring the demo scene with a real life scene and compare the two side by side, evaluating for realism.

Hopeful Deliverables

We hope to be able to simulate as well fluid interactions with bouyant materials realistically (i.e. ojects floating or sinking)

Again the quality of the system will be difficult to quantify, but a comparison between a modeled scene and real scene is a start

We also hope to be able to texture the fluid to appear as a body of liquid rather than the underlying particle representation

Schedule

Week 2 Complete underlying particle model and ensure particles interact as expected
Week 3 Add bouyancy and macro object interactions
